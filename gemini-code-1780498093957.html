The Periodic Table
Class 11 / JEE Core Base: Element Mafia ki Kahani

"Inorganic Chemistry ka map hai ye, bina iske rasta bhatak jaoge!"

Is presentation mein kya hai?
History (Dobereiner se Moseley tak), Modern Table ka Structure, aur sabse important: Periodic Trends aur unke Exceptions (JEE ke favorites!). Hum har trend ke peeche ki sachai (Zeff vs Shielding) ko decode karenge.

1. The Early Struggles: Dobereiner's Triads

Chemistry ka pehla "Jugaad". Dobereiner ne 3 elements ke group banaye aur bola: "Bhai, pehle aur teesre ka average mass nikal, beech wale ka mass aa jayega!"

The Hit & Flop:

  • Hit (Worked for): (Li, Na, K), (Ca, Sr, Ba), (Cl, Br, I).
  • Flop (Failed for): Pura table ispe set hi nahi ho paya. Fe, Co, Ni ne aukaat dikha di kyunki unka mass lagbhag same tha, average ka logic fail ho gaya.

*Le Elements: "Tu mere ko average samjha hai kya?"*

2. Newlands' Law of Octaves

John Newlands ko music ka bohot shauk tha. Usne elements ko increasing atomic mass mein lagaya aur bola: Har 8th element ki property 1st jaisi hogi (Sa-Re-Ga-Ma-Pa-Dha-Ni-Sa).

Where did the music stop?

  • Ye rule sirf Calcium (Ca) tak badiya chala (lighter elements).
  • Jaise hi d-block (transition elements) ki entry hui, 8th element ka logic toot gaya (ab 18 ke baad repeat hone laga).
  • Noble gases tab tak discover hi nahi hui thi, warna Sa-Re-Ga-Ma 8 pe nahi, 9 pe repeat hota!

3. The OG Predictor: Mendeleev's Table

Mendeleev "Chad" tha. Usne sirf elements ko arrange nahi kiya, balki jo elements discover nahi hue the, unke liye Khaali Jagah (Gaps) chhodi aur unki properties advance mein predict kar di!

Mendeleev ke "Eka" (One step below) Predictions:

  • Eka-Boron: Scandium (Sc)
  • Eka-Aluminium: Gallium (Ga) - (JEE PYQ favourite!)
  • Eka-Silicon: Germanium (Ge)

Drawbacks: Isotopes ki position explain nahi kar paya (sabka mass alag toh jagah bhi alag honi chahiye thi), aur Hydrogen ki position controversial rahi.

Slide 4 / 20

4. The Modern Savior: Henry Moseley (1913)

Moseley ne X-ray experiment kiya aur dekha ki element ki asal identity uska Atomic Mass nahi, balki Atomic Number (Z) hai (Number of Protons).

Modern Periodic Law

"The physical and chemical properties of the elements are periodic functions of their Atomic Numbers."

Is ek line ne Mendeleev ke saare faults (Isotopes problem, Ar-K inversion) ko ek jhatke mein fix kar diya. Kyunki mass aage-peeche ho sakta hai, par proton number humesha +1 se badhta hai.

Slide 5 / 20

5. Anatomy of the Modern Table

Table ko Blocks (s, p, d, f), Periods (Rows) aur Groups (Columns) mein divide kiya gaya hai.

  • 7 Periods: Principle quantum number (n) ko darshata hai. Row 1 (n=1) se Row 7 (n=7).
  • 18 Groups: Vertical columns. Same group = Same outer electronic configuration.
  • Magic Numbers: 2, 8, 8, 18, 18, 32. Ye numbers periods mein elements ki ginti hain, jo subshells (s, p, d, f) ke filling ke hisaab se aati hain.

7. Meet the Blocks: s-Block (The Highly Reactive)

Groups 1 aur 2. Ye log apna valence electron paani (water) mein dalte hi aag laga dete hain (literally). They just want to lose electrons and attain noble gas state.

  • Group 1 (Alkali Metals): ns1 configuration. Li, Na, K, Rb, Cs, Fr. Inke oxides water mein ghul kar strong base (alkali) banate hain.
  • Group 2 (Alkaline Earth Metals): ns2 configuration. Be, Mg, Ca, Sr, Ba, Ra. Earth's crust mein milte hain.
  • Key Feature: Bhot bade size ke hote hain, easily electron lose karte hain, isliye Strongest Reducing Agents hote hain.
Slide 8 / 20

8. p-Block (The Diverse Mafia)

Group 13 se 18. Ye akela block hai jisme Metals, Non-metals, aur Metalloids teeno milte hain. Ye chemistry ki sabse colorful aur dangerous family hai.

  • Group 15 (Pnictogens): N, P, As, Sb, Bi (Choking smell compounds).
  • Group 16 (Chalcogens): O, S, Se, Te, Po (Ore-forming).
  • Group 17 (Halogens): F, Cl, Br, I (Salt producers). Most electronegative.
  • Group 18 (Noble Gases): He, Ne, Ar, Kr, Xe, Rn. Sanyaasi log, unreactive (mostly), full octet.
Slide 9 / 20

9. d-Block & f-Block (The Heavyweights)

d-Block (Transition Elements):

Groups 3 to 12. "Transition" kyun? Kyunki ye s-block (highly electropositive) se p-block (electronegative) ke beech ka bridge hain. Inka (n-1)d orbital partially filled hota hai.
Vibes: Colorful compounds, Catalyst king, Variable oxidation states (+2, +3, +4... tak!). (Exception: Zn, Cd, Hg are NOT considered typical transition elements kyunki unka d-orbital full 3d10 hota hai).

f-Block (Inner-Transition Elements):

Table ke neeche chupaye gaye 2 rows. Lanthanoids (4f) aur Actinoids (5f - All radioactive). (n-2)f orbital fill hota hai.

Slide 10 / 20

10. The Real Boss: Effective Nuclear Charge (Zeff)

Agar tumhe Trends samajhne hain, toh Zeff samajhna padega. Ye Zeff hi sab bimariyon (exceptions) ki jadd hai.

Zeff = Z (Nuclear Charge) - σ (Screening/Shielding Constant)

  • Z (Nuclear Charge): Nucleus (Protons) ka aakhri electron ke liye "Pyar" (Attraction).
  • σ (Shielding): Andar wale electrons ka aakhri electron ko bahar dhakelna (Repulsion / "Dhokha").
  • Rule: s > p > d > f. d aur f orbitals ka shielding bohot hi WAHEEYAT (poor) hota hai. Wo nucleus ke attraction ko rok nahi paate, jisse outer electron andar kich jata hai!
Slide 11 / 20

11. Atomic Radius (Size of the Atom)

General Trend: Left se Right (Period mein) → Size Ghat ta hai. Top se Bottom (Group mein) → Size Badhta hai.

Why?

  • Left to Right: Shell wahi rehta hai, par protons (Z) badh rahe hain. Zeff badhta hai, electrons andar ki taraf zordaar pull hote hain. Shrinkage!
  • Top to Bottom: Naye shells (n) add ho rahe hain. Distance badh gaya, size badh gaya. Zeff lagbhag constant rehta hai group mein.
Slide 12 / 20

12. Size Exceptions (Jahan JEE khelta hai)

Group 13 ka Lafda: Aluminium (Al) vs Gallium (Ga)

Expectation: B < Al < Ga < In < Tl (Neeche aane par size badhna chahiye).

Reality: B < Ga < Al < In < Tl

Reason (Transition Contraction): Ga se pehle 3d series (Sc to Zn) aati hai. Inke d-electrons ka Poor Shielding Effect hota hai. Zeff badh jata hai, nucleus Ga ke outer electrons ko bohot zor se kichta hai, aur Ga ka size Al se thoda chhota ho jata hai! (Al=143 pm, Ga=135 pm).

Slide 13 / 20

13. Lanthanoid Contraction (The d-Block Twin Maker)

Jab hum 4d series se 5d series mein aate hain (e.g., Zr se Hf), toh size badhna chahiye. Par ek twist hai!

The 4f Mafia:

Hf (Hafnium, Z=72) se theek pehle 14 Lanthanoids (4f electrons) fill hote hain. f-electrons ka shielding "Sabse Ghatiya" hota hai. Is extreme poor shielding ki wajah se nucleus ka pull itna badh jata hai ki 5d series ka size shrink hoke lagbhag 4d series ke barabar ho jata hai.

Result: Zr (160 pm) ≈ Hf (159 pm). Inhe "Chemical Twins" bolte hain kyunki size same hone se properties bhi almost same ho jati hain!

Slide 14 / 20

14. Ionic Radius & Isoelectronic Species

Ions banne ke baad size kaise compare karein?

  • Cation (Positive Ion): Humesha apne parent atom se chhota hota hai. (Electron gaya, Zeff badha, size shrink hua. Na+ < Na)
  • Anion (Negative Ion): Humesha parent atom se bada hota hai. (Electron aaya, repulsion badha, Zeff ghata, size expand hua. Cl- > Cl)

Isoelectronic Series (Same no. of electrons)

Examples: N3-, O2-, F-, Na+, Mg2+, Al3+ (Sabke paas 10 electrons hain).

Trick: Jisme Protons (Z) sabse jyada, uska Size sabse CHHOTA! (More protons = Stronger pull).
Size Order: N3- > O2- > F- > Na+ > Mg2+ > Al3+

15. Ionization Enthalpy (IE)

Definition: Ek isolated gaseous atom se aakhri electron nikalne ke liye kitni energy (رشوت / Bribe) deni padegi. (Always Endothermic, ΔH > 0).

General Trend:

  • Left to Right (Increases): Size chhota ho raha hai, Zeff badh raha hai, nucleus electron ko tight pakad ke rakha hai. Nikalna mushkil (High IE).
  • Top to Bottom (Decreases): Size bada ho raha hai, electron nucleus se door hai. Thodi si energy do aur electron bahar (Low IE).

Successive IE: Humesha IE3 > IE2 > IE1. Kyunki positive ion se further electron nikalna bohot mushkil hota hai.

Slide 16 / 20

16. IE Exceptions (Half-filled & Fully-filled Magic)

JEE walon ko general trends se nafrat hai. Unhe exceptions chahiye!

Period 2 IE Order (Crucial)

Expectation: Li < Be < B < C < N < O < F < Ne

Reality: Li < B < Be < C < O < N < F < Ne

  • Be (2s2) vs B (2s2 2p1): Be ka s-orbital fully filled aur stable hai, s-orbital nucleus ke jyada paas (penetrated) hota hai. B ke p-electron ko nikalna aasan hai. So, IE of Be > B.
  • N (2p3) vs O (2p4): N exactly half-filled (stable) configuration mein hai. O apna ek electron dekar half-filled banna chahta hai. So, IE of N > O.
Slide 17 / 20

17. Electron Gain Enthalpy (EGE or ΔegH)

Definition: Jab ek isolated gaseous atom ek extra electron accept karta hai, toh kitni energy release hoti hai. (Usually Exothermic, ΔH is negative). Halogens ko electron ka sabse jyada lalach hota hai (Highest negative EGE).

General Trend vs Exception (The Trap!):

General trend kehta hai ki F ki EGE sabse jyada negative honi chahiye. Par sachai alag hai!

Reality: Chlorine (Cl) > Fluorine (F) (Magnitude mein)

Reason: F ka size bohot bohot chhota hai aur 2p subshell mein pehle se 7 electrons congested hain. Naya electron aayega toh existing electrons usko bohot repel karenge (Inter-electronic repulsion). Cl ka 3p subshell bada hai, room khali hai, naya electron aaram se adjust ho jata hai.

Slide 18 / 20

18. Electronegativity (EN) - The Electron Chor

Definition: Covalent bond mein shared pair of electrons ko apni taraf kheenchna. Ye koi absolute energy nahi hai, bas ek tendency (aadat) hai.

The FON Rule (Most Electronegative Mafia):

Pauling Scale pe highest values:

  • Fluorine (F) = 4.0 (Sabse bada chor)
  • Oxygen (O) = 3.5
  • Nitrogen (N) = Chlorine (Cl) = 3.0

Trend: Left to Right → Increases. Top to Bottom → Decreases. (Isme koi major shielding wala exception nahi hai! Fluorine dominates strictly).

19. Final Pro-Tips: Diagonal Rel. & Inert Pair Effect

Diagonal Relationship:

2nd period ka element, 3rd period ke diagonally opposite element se properties match karta hai.
(Li & Mg), (Be & Al), (B & Si).
Reason: Unka Size (radius) aur Charge/Size (Ionic potential/Polarizing power) ratio lagbhag same hota hai.

Inert Pair Effect (Teaser for p-Block):

p-block mein neeche jane par (like Tl, Pb, Bi), s-orbital ke 2 electrons bond banane se mana kar dete hain (They become INERT). Kyun? Wahi purana villain: Poor shielding of d and f electrons makes nucleus pull the s-electrons very tightly. Isliye Pb(+2) is more stable than Pb(+4).
